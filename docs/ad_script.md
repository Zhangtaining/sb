# SmartGym AI — Advertisement Video Script

**Title:** *Every Rep Counts*
**Format:** Brand hero film / product demo hybrid
**Length:** 3:00–3:30 (long-form for web/social) — edit down to :90 and :30 cuts
**Tone:** Cinematic, warm, aspirational. Not tech-cold. This is about a *human* feeling empowered, not a machine being impressive.
**Music Direction:** Starts ambient and intimate (soft piano + light synth breath). Builds gradually into a confident, pulse-driven instrumental (think Hans Zimmer light — not aggressive EDM). Final third lifts into something emotionally swelling without being cheesy.
**Color Grade:** Warm tungsten gym lighting in early scenes. Progressively brighter and more saturated as the character gains confidence. Final scene: clean, golden-hour light wash.

---

## CAST

- **MARCUS** — Male, late 20s / early 30s. Athletic build but visibly self-conscious. Gym regular who's been showing up but not seeing results. Relatable, not a model.
- **V.O. / AI COACH VOICE** — Female, calm and warm. Never robotic. Think a very good human coach who happens to be always available. Delivered via earphone; slightly intimate spatial audio mix.

## SCENE-BY-SCENE BREAKDOWN

---

### SCENE 01 — "Before" — 0:00–0:20

**Location:** Exterior of a mid-premium gym. Early morning. Street still quiet. Warm interior light spills through the glass facade.

**Action:**
Marcus stands outside the entrance, gym bag over one shoulder, AirPods in. He looks through the glass for a beat — watching other members already training inside. His expression is a mix of determination and quiet doubt. He exhales. Pushes the door open.

**Camera:**
- Opens on a low wide shot of the gym exterior. Camera is static. Marcus walks into frame from the left, pauses at the door.
- CUT TO: tight over-the-shoulder shot from behind Marcus, looking through the glass at the gym floor — cameras mounted on the ceiling are subtly visible (don't linger, just plant the seed).
- CUT TO: low-angle close-up of his hand pushing the door open. The glass door reflects the street behind him as it swings open.

**Audio:**
- Ambient urban morning sound. A distant bus. Wind.
- Music: single low piano note. Holds. Doesn't resolve yet.
- No dialogue.

**On-screen text:** None yet.

**Visual notes:** The gym cameras should be visible on the ceiling through the glass — but the audience won't know what they mean yet. This is a plant. It pays off in Scene 04.

---

### SCENE 02 — "Check-In" — 0:20–0:45

**Location:** Gym reception / entrance zone. Clean, modern. A simple SmartGym AI branded kiosk tablet near the turnstile.

**Action:**
Marcus approaches the kiosk. He doesn't scan a card or type anything. He just walks up — and the screen blinks to life. His face is recognized in under a second. The screen displays:

> **"Good morning, Marcus."**
> *"Last session: Tuesday — 3 sets of squats, 2 sets of rows. Left knee flagged for form review."*
> *"Ready to start?"*

Marcus glances at the screen with mild surprise — it remembered. He taps "Let's go." Walks through the turnstile.

As he passes, a subtle **green pulse animation** ripples outward from the overhead camera — visible to the audience but not to Marcus. A thin wireframe silhouette briefly overlays his body on-screen (split-second AR overlay, like the system just "acquired" him).

**Camera:**
- MEDIUM SHOT: Marcus walking toward the kiosk, camera tracks him at eye height, slow lateral drift left-to-right.
- CUT TO: CLOSE-UP of the kiosk screen — face recognition animation (a soft circular scan ring, not sci-fi laser — subtle and friendly). Text appears.
- CUT TO: CLOSE-UP on Marcus's face reading the screen. A small smile — it got something right.
- CUT TO: WIDE OVERHEAD SHOT (from ceiling camera POV): Marcus taps the screen, walks through turnstile. The AR wireframe overlay activates around his silhouette. Hold 1 second, then cut.

**Audio:**
- A soft, pleasant two-tone chime as the screen activates.
- Music lifts very slightly — a second piano note joins the first. Still restrained.
- No voice yet.

**On-screen text (kiosk UI):**
```
Good morning, Marcus.
Last session: Tuesday
Left knee — form review flagged.
Ready to start?       [Let's go →]
```

**Visual notes:** The kiosk UI should look like a real product, not a concept mockup. Clean sans-serif font. Dark background. The recognition animation should feel instant — not a long dramatic scan.

---

### SCENE 03 — "Earphones In" — 0:45–1:00

**Location:** Gym floor entryway. Marcus sets down his bag at a bench near the edge of the floor. He takes out his phone, opens the SmartGym AI app. A session auto-starts.

**Action:**
Marcus opens the app. The interface shows:

> *AI Coach is connecting…*
> *(Session started — Marcus, Floor Zone B)*

He adjusts his AirPods. A beat of silence. Then the AI voice speaks directly to him — warm, calm, unhurried:

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Hey Marcus. Good to have you back. You hit 60-kilo squats on Tuesday — how are the legs feeling today?"*

Marcus smirks slightly — half charmed, half weirded out. He speaks quietly:

> **MARCUS:**
> *"A bit sore, honestly."*

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Okay — let's do upper body today and come back to legs Thursday. I've got a pull-push session ready for you. Want to start with rows?"*

Marcus nods slightly, pockets the phone.

**Camera:**
- CLOSE-UP: phone screen showing the app UI. Thumb taps to open session.
- CUT TO: medium shot — Marcus adjusting AirPods, looking slightly self-conscious in the way people do when they're about to talk to an AI in public.
- HOLD on his face as the AI speaks. We hear it clearly (slight earphone audio quality filter to signal it's coming through earphones).
- CUT TO: CLOSE-UP of his hands pocketing the phone. He's ready.

**Audio:**
- The AI voice is key. It must never sound like Siri. It should sound like a coach who texted you the night before to ask how your knee was doing.
- His reply is barely above a whisper. Real. Not performed.
- Music: a third layer enters — soft pad, barely there.

**On-screen text:** App UI visible briefly. Session status card: "Marcus — Upper Body Pull/Push | 5 exercises | ~55 min"

---

### SCENE 04 — "The System Sees Everything" — 1:00–1:30

**Location:** Gym floor. Marcus walks to the cable row station. The gym is moderately busy — other members in background.

**Action:**
This scene is the **visual centrepiece** — it reveals how the system works without a single line of explanation.

As Marcus sets up at the cable machine, the camera pulls back to reveal a **split-screen or augmented reality overlay visualization**:

- On one side (or overlaid in a stylized way): the raw camera footage from the ceiling-mounted SmartGym AI camera, showing Marcus at the station.
- Overlaid on his body: colored pose skeleton lines — shoulders, elbows, wrists lit up. A small floating label: *"Seated Cable Row — tracking"*.
- Rep counter appears: **0 reps**.

Marcus begins rowing. With each rep, the skeleton lines pulse. The rep counter increments: 1 … 2 … 3 …

On his third rep, the right elbow angle indicator flickers amber. On the fifth rep it goes red. A subtle vibration on his phone (wrist-height visible in shot).

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Marcus — your right elbow is flaring a bit on the pull. Drive the elbow down, not back. Like you're trying to put it in your back pocket."*

Marcus adjusts. The elbow angle indicator returns to green. The skeleton lines look clean. He continues — reps 6, 7, 8.

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Much better. That's it."*

**Camera:**
- Opens with a MEDIUM WIDE SHOT of Marcus setting up. Camera is at floor level, slightly below eye line, looking up — makes him look capable.
- SLOW DOLLY IN toward him as he begins his first reps.
- CUT TO: OVERHEAD CAMERA POV (stylized — like looking at a surveillance feed but beautifully lit). The skeleton overlay appears. This is the "reveal" of the technology.
- INTERCUT between: overhead skeleton view | close-up of his face concentrating | close-up of the elbow joint (from side) showing the angle going amber/red | his face reacting to the earphone cue | close-up of elbow correcting — angle returning green.
- After the correction, PULL BACK to a wide shot showing Marcus in the context of the whole gym. He's just one person — but the system is watching carefully.

**Audio:**
- Gym ambient sounds: cable machine clicks, distant weights, other members.
- The AI voice correction is intimate — heard only through earphones. Sound design: slight spatial effect, like it's coming from his left ear.
- Music: the pad layer grows slightly. A low rhythmic pulse begins underneath.

**On-screen text (overlaid AR style, minimal):**
```
Seated Cable Row
Rep: 5 / 12
Right elbow angle: 142° ⚠  [→ 118° ✓]
Form score: 74% → 91%
```

**Visual notes:** This is NOT a PowerPoint diagram. The skeleton overlay should feel like something from a nature documentary — the way BBC Earth animates animal skeletons. Elegant, not clinical. The amber/red/green color shift must be clear but not alarm-like.

### SCENE 05 — "Between Sets — The Conversation" — 1:30–2:00

**Location:** Same station. Marcus sits back between sets. He takes a breath. Grabs his water bottle. He speaks to his phone quietly (it's on the bench beside him):

**Action:**

> **MARCUS:**
> *"How many sets did I do of this last time?"*

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Two sets, 10 reps each at 45 kilos. You hit all your reps cleanly. Want to try 50 today?"*

Marcus considers. Nods.

> **MARCUS:**
> *"Yeah. Let's try it."*

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Good call. Rest timer's running — you've got about 40 seconds. I'll remind you."*

The phone screen (visible in shot) shows a circular rest timer counting down. Clean UI. 40 … 39 … 38.

Marcus looks around the gym for a moment. Relaxed. Like he's with someone who has his back.

**Camera:**
- CLOSE-UP on Marcus's face during the exchange — natural, conversational. Not dramatic.
- CUT TO: CLOSE-UP of phone screen showing the rest timer UI.
- PULL BACK slowly to a MEDIUM WIDE SHOT. Marcus sitting at the cable station, phone on the bench beside him. The shot holds for a few seconds. He looks calm and focused — not lost or uncertain like most solo gym-goers.
- SLOW PUSH IN on his face as the timer counts down. Subtle determination.

**Audio:**
- The AI conversation should feel genuinely natural. The voice has memory. It knows him.
- Ambient gym sound slightly lower in this scene — more intimate headspace.
- Music: the rhythmic pulse pauses. Just the pad. Rest.

**On-screen text (app UI visible):**
```
Rest Timer: 0:38
Next: Set 2 — Cable Row
Target: 50 kg × 10 reps
```

### SCENE 06 — "Personal Best" — 2:00–2:20

**Location:** Later in the session. Marcus is at the pull-down station. He's visibly working harder — the weight is heavier.

**Action:**
He grinds out rep 9. Rep 10. Pauses. Then — with a deep breath — pulls one more. Rep 11. He's never done 11 at this weight.

A beat of silence.

Then the phone on the bench **lights up**. A warm pulsing glow. The app shows a **"Personal Best"** card — animated, celebratory but not cheesy. The number **11** prominent on screen.

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"That's a new personal best, Marcus. 11 reps at 65 kilos. That's six weeks of consistent work paying off."*

Marcus sets the bar down. Sits back. Exhales. He's breathing hard. He looks at the phone screen. Something shifts in his expression — not just satisfaction, but *proof*. This mattered.

**Camera:**
- TIGHT on his hands gripping the bar. Knuckles white.
- INTERCUT rapid cuts during the final reps: face straining | bar moving | skeleton rep counter ticking | face | bar | counter.
- Then — sudden cut to STILLNESS. Wide shot. He lets the bar up. Silence for half a beat.
- SLOW ZOOM IN on the phone screen lighting up. The "Personal Best" animation.
- CUT TO: CLOSE-UP of Marcus's face. The exhale. The look. Hold this for 3 full seconds — no rushing it. This is the emotional core of the ad.

**Audio:**
- The final reps: real effort sounds — breath, exertion, cable mechanism.
- When the PB is confirmed: a single warm chime. Not a celebration fanfare. A resonant bell tone. Simple and meaningful.
- The AI voice is softer here. Not triumphant — *proud*.
- Music: the piano returns. Cleaner now. The pad swells underneath it. This is the emotional peak building.

**On-screen text (app UI — full screen moment):**
```
        🏆 Personal Best
        Lat Pulldown — 65 kg
        11 reps  (prev. best: 10)
        
"Six weeks of consistent work."
```

### SCENE 07 — "Wider World — The Gym Operator" — 2:20–2:35

**Location:** Gym manager's office. Adjacent to the floor — glass wall looking out. A woman (gym owner/manager, 40s, professional but approachable) is at her desk reviewing a tablet.

**Action:**
The tablet shows the SmartGym AI **operator dashboard**: a top-down floor map of the gym with real-time member positions (anonymized dots). Equipment usage heatmap. A small notification:

> *"3 members approaching personal bests this session."*

She smiles — not at a screen, but at what it means. She looks through the glass wall at the gym floor — at Marcus, and others training. She sees the results of something working.

**Camera:**
- MEDIUM SHOT: operator at desk, tablet in hand. Camera slowly drifts in.
- CUT TO: CLOSE-UP of tablet screen showing the dashboard — floor map, heatmap, notification.
- CUT TO: her POV looking through the glass wall at the gym floor. We see members training — including Marcus at his station.
- SLOW RACK FOCUS from the glass (foreground) to the gym floor (background). Her reflection fades as the floor comes into focus.

**Audio:**
- Music continues to build.
- No dialogue in this scene. Pure visual storytelling.
- Ambient sound: muffled gym noise through the glass.

**On-screen text (dashboard UI):**
```
SmartGym AI — Live Floor View
Active members: 23    Avg form score: 84%
Peak zone: Free weights
⭐ 3 members approaching personal bests
```

**Visual notes:** This scene should be brief — 12–15 seconds. Its purpose is to show the operator perspective without making the ad feel like enterprise software. The emotional thread is: *"She's watching her members succeed."* Not: *"She's monitoring data."*

### SCENE 08 — "End of Session" — 2:35–2:55

**Location:** Gym floor. Marcus is wrapping up — towel around his neck, collecting his things.

**Action:**
The app shows a session summary card. Marcus glances at it while walking toward the exit.

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Great session today. 5 exercises, 38 total reps, form score 88%. Sleep well — Thursday, legs."*

Marcus chuckles quietly. He pushes the door open to leave. As he steps out into the (now warmer, sunnier) street — he stands taller. Different from when he arrived.

**Camera:**
- CLOSE-UP of phone screen — session summary card slides in.
- MEDIUM SHOT of Marcus collecting his bag, towel around neck, relaxed and spent.
- TRACKING SHOT following Marcus from behind as he walks toward the exit. Low angle. His stride is different — more settled.
- He pushes open the door — MATCH CUT to the same door-opening shot from Scene 01, but now in reverse perspective: from inside looking out, morning light now fuller and warmer.
- HOLD on Marcus walking away down the street. Camera stays inside. He gets smaller as he moves into the morning light. Then he's gone.

**Audio:**
- AI voice: casual, warm, like a coach texting after practice.
- Music: the build peaks here — full, warm, resolved. Not triumphant. Quietly confident.
- Ambient sound fades as door closes.

**On-screen text (app UI):**
```
Session Complete — Marcus
Duration: 58 min
Exercises: 5  |  Sets: 14  |  Reps: 38
Form Score: 88%  ▲ +7% from last session
Next session: Thursday — Legs Day
```

### SCENE 09 — "Title Card" — 2:55–3:10

**Location:** Black screen.

**Action:**
Pure black. Silence for 1 second.

Then — very simply — white text appears, one line at a time, with the same quiet confidence the ad has built toward:

```
Every gym member deserves a great coach.
```

*(2-second hold)*

```
Now every gym can offer one.
```

*(2-second hold)*

**SmartGym AI logo** — clean wordmark, no tagline clutter.

*(1-second hold)*

Then a final line below the logo:

```
smartgym.ai
For gym operators — book a demo.
```

**Camera:** N/A — static card.

**Audio:**
- The final piano note from the musical theme. Sustained. Fades to silence.
- No voiceover. Let the words breathe.

**Visual notes:** Do NOT rush the title card. Advertisers always cut it too short. These words need at least 6–7 full seconds on screen. The viewer has just watched 3 minutes of cinema — give the message space to land.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Locations
- Primary gym location should look **premium but real** — not a pristine white studio. Members in background, real equipment, real lighting. If it looks like a stock photo, it's wrong.
- Consider shooting in a real gym outside business hours for controlled lighting, then adding BG artists.

### Camera Package
- Principal photography: ARRI ALEXA Mini LF or Sony FX9 with a set of Zeiss CP.3 lenses (35mm, 50mm, 85mm)
- Ceiling POV shots: RED Komodo in a small housing or GoPro with LOG footage, color-matched in post
- Gimbal: DJI RS3 Pro for tracking shots
- Macro/detail lens (35mm close-focus or dedicated macro): for knuckle / phone screen / joint angle close-ups

### Skeleton Overlay / AR Graphics
- Shot-matched in post using 3D tracking (SynthEyes or PFTrack)
- Skeleton should be animated slightly — not perfectly rigid. Breathing motion. The lines should feel *alive*, not drawn.
- Color palette for skeleton: default white/light blue lines; amber (#F59E0B) for warning; red (#EF4444) for error; green (#10B981) for correct. All consistent with SmartGym AI brand.
- The skeleton should *appear* to come from the ceiling camera, not from a different angle. The perspective of the overlay must match the ceiling POV shots.

### App UI Design
- The app UI seen on screen must be real (or identical to real). Build the actual UI in the app before shooting, and use a real device with real content. Nothing breaks trust faster than a clearly mocked-up phone screen.
- The rest timer, session summary, and PB card should all be real, running UI — not static images.

### On-Set AI Voice
- Record and finalize the AI voice *before* the shoot. The actor needs to react to the actual line, not a placeholder. Play it through earphones on set and capture his real reaction in real time.
- Voice casting: audition 6–8 voice actors. Do NOT use a synthesized TTS voice — use a real human voice actor. Post-production can add the very lightest processing (clarity, warmth) but the foundation must be human.

### Sound Design (Post)
- The gym ambient sound should feel textural and real — not generically "gym busy." Specific sounds: the click of a cable machine selector pin, the soft thud of a bumper plate being set down gently, a distant conversation, someone's shoes on rubber matting.
- The earphone audio effect: when the AI voice is heard, apply a very subtle bandpass EQ and a 2ms room size — replicating what a premium in-ear would sound like. Not a phone speaker. This signals intimacy.

### Casting Note — Marcus
- Do not cast someone who looks like a fitness model or Instagram athlete. The audience for this ad is *gym operators*. They are imagining their *members* using this product. The more "average but committed" Marcus looks, the more the operator thinks: *"Yes, this is my member."*
- The performance direction for Marcus: *restraint*. His reactions to the AI should be small. A half-smile, not a grin. A nod, not a thumbs-up. Real people don't emote theatrically at technology.

## DIRECTOR'S STATEMENT

The biggest risk in advertising an AI product is making it feel cold — like the technology is the hero. This film deliberately inverts that. **Marcus is the hero. The AI is his coach.** The camera is always on him — his hands, his face, his breathing. The technology is revealed through his experience of it, not through explainer graphics or feature lists.

The emotional arc is simple: **a person who shows up alone, leaves less alone.** Not because he made a friend, but because for the first time, someone (something) in the gym was actually paying attention to him. That feeling — of being seen and coached — is what SmartGym AI sells. Everything else is delivery mechanism.

Every frame should ask: *"Does this feel like a person being helped?"* If the answer is yes, keep it. If it feels like a product demo, cut it.
