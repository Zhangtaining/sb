# SmartGym AI — Short-Form Ad Script (45s)

**Title:** *Your Gym. Every Member. One AI Coach.*
**Format:** Punchy product pitch — built for gym owners
**Length:** 45 seconds (hard stop)
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 primary; safe for 9:16 (Reels/TikTok) crop
**Tone:** Direct, confident, smart. No fluff. Every second earns its place.
**Target Viewer:** Gym operator / owner — watching a pre-roll or a sales deck embed. They are busy. Win them in the first 5 seconds or lose them.
**Music:** Single unbroken track. Opens with a low, forward-moving pulse (no melody yet). First lyrical/melodic moment hits exactly when the skeleton overlay appears at 0:12. Builds to a clean, resolved swell at 0:38. Out by 0:45.
**Color Grade:** Consistent warm-neutral throughout — no dramatic arc like the long version. Clean, premium, trustworthy.

---

## CAST

- **MEMBER** — Male or female, 28–35. Gym-regular look. No voice lines.
- **AI COACH VOICE** — Female. Warm, precise. Two lines only.
- **GYM OWNER** — Brief cameo. No lines. Watching a dashboard on a tablet.

## FULL SCENE BREAKDOWN

---

### SHOT 01 — The Problem (0:00–0:06)

**Duration:** 6 seconds

**Location:** Gym floor. Wide shot. A dozen members training — cables, free weights, benches. Busy and real.

**Action:**
Camera holds on the wide shot. Everything looks normal. Then a subtle on-screen graphic fades in over specific members one by one — a small amber icon above each person:

> ⚠ *No guidance*
> ⚠ *No guidance*
> ⚠ *No guidance*

Every single member on the floor. All training alone. All without feedback.

**Camera:**
- STATIC WIDE SHOT — no movement. Let the icons do the work.
- The icons should appear one by one, staggered 0.3s apart, left to right across the frame. Subtle fade-in, not a pop.

**Audio:**
- Gym ambient sound. Real. No music yet.
- No voiceover.

**On-screen text:**
```
⚠  No guidance     ⚠  No guidance     ⚠  No guidance
        ⚠  No guidance          ⚠  No guidance
```
*(Overlaid on each member, floating above their heads, amber)*

**Director's note:** This shot should feel almost uncomfortably quiet. No music is intentional — the silence makes the problem feel real. The audience (gym owners) should think: *"That's my floor."*

---

### SHOT 02 — The System Activates (0:06–0:12)

**Duration:** 6 seconds

**Location:** Ceiling-mounted SmartGym AI camera. Close-up on the lens. A soft LED indicator blinks to green.

**Action:**
Extreme close-up of the camera housing. The indicator light blinks once — amber — then holds green. The lens is sharp, purposeful. This is the moment the system turns on.

**Camera:**
- EXTREME CLOSE-UP on the camera lens. Rack focus from housing to the lens itself.
- Hold 2 seconds. Then —
- CUT TO: OVERHEAD POV from the camera, looking down at the gym floor. The frame fills with the top-down view of members training. The floor map grid fades in over the image.

**Audio:**
- A single electronic *click* as the LED goes green.
- Music: the pulse begins. Low, forward-moving. Builds underneath the next shot.

**On-screen text:** None.

---

### SHOT 03 — AI Sees Every Member (0:12–0:22)

**Duration:** 10 seconds ← *the visual centrepiece*

**Location:** Gym floor. The Member is at a cable row station.

**Action:**
The Member begins a set. As they pull the cable, a **pose skeleton overlay** appears on their body — elegant colored lines tracing joints in real time. The rep counter ticks up in the corner of frame: **1 … 2 … 3 …**

On rep 4, the right elbow angle turns **amber**. On rep 5 — **red**.

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"Drive the elbow down — like you're putting it in your back pocket."*

The Member adjusts. The elbow indicator flicks to **green** instantly. The skeleton looks clean. Reps continue: 6 … 7 … 8.

The amber icons from Shot 01 are now **gone**. In their place: a small green checkmark above each member on the floor.

**Camera:**
- Opens on MEDIUM SHOT of the Member starting their set. Camera at floor level, slightly below eye line.
- As skeleton overlay appears: SLOW PUSH IN toward the member.
- INTERCUT (rapid, 1s each): elbow joint close-up going amber → red | face reacting to earphone cue | elbow correcting → green.
- CUT BACK TO wide shot: all members on the floor now have the green checkmark overlay.

**Audio:**
- The AI voice correction lands cleanly through the earphone audio filter (warm, slightly intimate, not broadcast-y).
- Music lifts — the melodic layer enters here.

**On-screen text (AR overlay, minimal):**
```
Seated Cable Row    Rep: 5 / 12
Right elbow: ⚠ 142°  →  ✓ 118°
```

---

### SHOT 04 — Personal Best (0:22–0:30)

**Duration:** 8 seconds

**Location:** Same gym floor. The Member finishing a set — visibly pushing hard on the last rep.

**Action:**
They grind out one final rep. Set the weight down. A beat.

The **phone screen on the bench lights up** with a warm pulse:

```
🏆  Personal Best
Lat Pulldown — 65 kg  |  11 reps
```

> **AI VOICE (V.O.):**
> *"New personal best. That's six weeks of work paying off."*

The Member looks at the screen. A small exhale. A look that says: *this is working.*

**Camera:**
- TIGHT on hands releasing the bar.
- CUT TO: phone screen lighting up (close-up). The PB card animation.
- CUT TO: CLOSE-UP of the Member's face. Hold 2 full seconds. No rush.

**Audio:**
- A single warm bell tone on the PB card appearing.
- AI voice: softer here. Not triumphant — *proud*.
- Music swells underneath.

**On-screen text (phone UI):**
```
🏆  Personal Best
Lat Pulldown — 65 kg
11 reps  (prev. 10)
```

### SHOT 05 — The Operator View (0:30–0:37)

**Duration:** 7 seconds

**Location:** Gym manager's office. Glass wall overlooking the floor.

**Action:**
The gym owner holds a tablet showing the **SmartGym AI dashboard**: a live floor map, equipment heatmap, member engagement scores. A notification reads:

> *"3 members hit personal bests this session."*

She looks up from the tablet — through the glass — at the gym floor. Her expression is quiet satisfaction. She's not watching a tech product. She's watching her members succeed.

**Camera:**
- MEDIUM SHOT of the owner, tablet in hand, glass wall behind her.
- CLOSE-UP on the dashboard notification.
- RACK FOCUS through the glass: foreground (her reflection) softens as the gym floor behind it sharpens. Members training. The Member visible at their station.

**Audio:**
- Music at its fullest — resolved, warm.
- No dialogue.

**On-screen text (dashboard):**
```
SmartGym AI  |  Live View
Active: 23 members    Avg form score: 84%
⭐ 3 members hit personal bests this session
```

### SHOT 06 — Title Card (0:37–0:45)

**Duration:** 8 seconds

**Location:** Black.

**Action:**
Clean black. Text appears in sequence:

*(0:37)* — First line fades in, centered, white:
```
Your gym. Every member. One AI coach.
```

*(0:40)* — Logo appears below the line:
```
SmartGym AI
```

*(0:42)* — CTA appears below logo:
```
Book a demo →  smartgym.ai
```

**Camera:** Static. No movement.

**Audio:**
- Music resolves to a single held note. Fades to silence by 0:44.
- No voiceover.

## PRODUCTION NOTES (SHORT VERSION SPECIFIC)

- **Shot 01 is the boldest choice in this cut.** Starting with silence and a problem — no music, no hero — is a risk. But gym owners will recognise their floor immediately. If you flinch and add music here, you lose the gut-punch. Hold the silence.
- **The amber → red → green elbow correction in Shot 03 must be unmistakable.** Use high-contrast colors. If a viewer watching on their phone at 50% brightness can't read the color shift, it's wasted. Consider adding a thin white ring around the joint indicator to help it pop off any background.
- **The owner in Shot 05 should look like an owner, not a model.** Real gym owners are 38–55, practical clothes, pragmatic expression. Casting a 28-year-old in athletic wear sends the wrong signal.
- **Music brief for composer/music supervisor:** The track must work as one unbroken piece across all 45 seconds. No jarring section transitions. Think of it as three stages: (1) 0:00–0:06 silence / no music, (2) 0:06–0:37 building pulse with a melodic swell peaking at 0:30, (3) 0:37–0:45 resolution and fade. Reference: Nils Frahm, *Says* — the slow-build layering approach.
- **This cut lives and dies on Shot 03.** If the skeleton overlay looks cheap or the correction demo is unconvincing, the whole product claim falls apart. Invest post-production time here first. The rest of the cut can be rough — Shot 03 cannot.
